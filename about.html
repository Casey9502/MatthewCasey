About Me

Discover my journey, skills, and passion for creativity and technology.

My Creative Journey

Age 5 – First Guitar

I picked up a guitar for the first time and began lessons. Over the next 10 years, I developed a deep foundation in music, rhythm, and creativity through consistent practice and exploration.

Age 15–18 – Bedroom Producer

I started recording tracks on my phone, experimenting with vocals, beats, and layering sounds using basic apps. It was lo-fi, raw, and authentic — the spark of audio storytelling took hold.

Age 18 – University of Limerick

I joined UL to study Music, Media & Performance Technology. I dove into sound design, coding, video production, and interactive media. It transformed my skill set and expanded my creative toolkit.

Now – Future in Film & Audio

At 22, I'm channeling everything into a future in film and audio. My passion is immersive storytelling — blending technology and emotion through sound. The journey is just getting started.

Why Music Technology?

What drew me to this field was the intersection of artistic expression and technical innovation — where musical creativity meets problem-solving. At the University of Limerick, I discovered how sound design can shape stories not just in music, but in film, games, and interactive installations.

This realization transformed my perspective: audio is more than just what we hear — it's how we feel, navigate, and experience media. Today, I continue to explore this balance through work in spatial audio, multimedia production, and experimental sound design. The same curiosity that pulled me toward music tech still drives me to push boundaries and create immersive, emotionally resonant experiences.

Beyond the Studio

When I’m not sculpting sounds or editing footage, I’m often hiking trails with a field recorder in hand, playing strategy games, or digging through thrift shops for obscure records and vintage gear. These interests — from the rhythm of nature to the structure of games — constantly feed into my creative process, shaping how I think about sound, space, and story.
